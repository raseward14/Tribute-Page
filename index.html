The 1975

The band redefining todays music.

Here's a time line of The 1975:

  • 2021 - Lead singer Matty Healy eluded to future music under the name Drive Like I Do, and said that the band was working on their fifth studio album.
  • 2019-2021 - Single, Frail State of Mind is released on October 24th 2019. The bands fourth album, Notes on a Conditional Form, was released on May 22nd 2020, reaching number 4 in the United States, number 1 in Australia, and number 1 on the UK Albums Chart.
  • 2017-2019 - Won British Album of the Year at the 2019 Brit Awards for A Brief Inquiry into Online Relationships
  • 2015-2017 - On October 8th, 2015 the band announced album #2 I Like it When You Sleep, for You Are So Beautiful yet so Unaware of it containing Love Me, UGH!, The Sound and Somebody Else. They are nominated the the 2017 Brit Awards, and listed on the US Billboard 200.
  • 2012-2014 - The band releases their self titled debut album. It tops the UK Albums Chart, including singlesThe City, Sex, and Chocolate reaching number 19 in the UK Singles Chart. On May 20 2013 the band released IV including a new version of The City.
  • 2002-2011 - Lead singer Matthew Healy meets Ross MacDonald, Adam Hann and George Daniel at Wilmslow High School in 2002, and they begin to play together as teenagers. They settle on the name The 1975 inspired by scribbles found on the backpage of the book On The Road by Jack Kerouac that were dated 'June 1, The 1975'.
